Writing and Editing

One theme connecting my writing is finding one's way. Both my poetry and my academic research explore the idea of maps and compasses, of knowing where you are by considering where you have been and where you might be going.

My first sole-authored academic book, Distant Readings of Disciplinarity: Knowing and Doing in Composition​/​Rhetoric Dissertations, maps the methods and content of several thousand dissertations on rhetoric, composition, writing, and literacy, 2001–​2015. It was published in December 2022 by Utah State University Press as the inaugural book in their Writing Research, Pedagogy, and Policy series.

I am the coeditor with Dr. Amanda Licastro (University of Pennsylvania) of the book-length collection Composition as Big Data, published in Fall 2021 by University of Pittsburgh Press as part of their series in Composition, Literacy, and Culture. The book aims to kickstart a collective reckoning in RCWS with the role that algorithmic and computational approaches can, or should, play in our research and teaching.

I published my first collection of poetry, Without Compass, with Four Way Books in 2014. Poet Tom Healy described the book this way: “In his beautiful, bruised new book, Without Compass, Benjamin Miller presses questions on us about the risks of losing our bearings in the world of feeling. And his poems hurl us into new territory, answers that are somehow both brutal and comforting…. These gorgeous, painful poems map the unpredictable weather of the psyche: torrential, scorching, cold or calm. We would be lost without them.”

Building and Developing

In addition to various documentation efforts on university committees, I am interested in developing tools and resources that help orient scholars within complex disciplinary spaces.

The Writing Studies Tree is an online, crowdsourced database of academic genealogies within writing studies; in other words, it is an interactive archive for recording and mapping scholarly relationships in Composition and Rhetoric and adjacent disciplines.

The Journal of Interactive Technology and Pedagogy (JITP) promotes open scholarly discourse around critical and creative uses of technology in teaching, learning, and research. I am proud to have been a member of the editorial collective since before the first issue launched in 2012, and I have worked in various editorial and production roles ever since. Most recently, I am lead editor of issue 22, forthcoming in late spring 2023.
